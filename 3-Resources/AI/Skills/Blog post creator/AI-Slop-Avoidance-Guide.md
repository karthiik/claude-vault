# AI Slop Avoidance Guide
## How to Write Content That Doesn't Scream "ChatGPT Wrote This"

*Last updated: January 2026*

---

## What is "AI Slop"?

"AI slop" (2025 Word of the Year per Merriam-Webster) refers to digital content made with generative AI that is perceived as lacking effort, quality, or deeper meaning, produced at overwhelming volume. The term carries the same pejorative weight as "spam."

Related terms:
- **Slopper**: Someone overly reliant on generative AI tools
- **Glazing**: Excessive or insincere praise (a hallmark of ChatGPT's sycophantic tendencies)

---

## The Big Picture: Why Detection Matters

Research shows:
- 54% of longer English-language LinkedIn posts are AI-written
- Consumer enthusiasm for AI-generated creator work dropped from 60% (2023) to 26% (2025)
- 53% of people report being annoyed by AI-produced "workslop," 38% confused, 22% offended

## PHRASES TO AVOID

### Opening Lines (The Worst)

Never start with:
- "In today's [fast-paced/ever-evolving/dynamic] world..."
- "In the [landscape/realm/world] of..."
- "Picture this..."
- "Imagine a world where..."
- "Let's dive into..."
- "Let's delve into..."
- "Let's unpack..."
- "As we navigate..."
- "In an era of..."
- "In the dynamic world of..."

## PUNCTUATION AND FORMATTING

### The Em Dash Problem

**The Issue**: AI overuses em dashes (â€”) where humans would use commas, parentheses, or colons. It's become so associated with ChatGPT that some call it the "ChatGPT hyphen."

**The Reality**: Em dashes are legitimate punctuation that humans use too. The problem is:
1. AI uses them too frequently
2. AI uses them in formulaic, predictable ways
3. AI uses them where other punctuation would be more natural

**What to do**:
- Don't avoid em dashes entirely, but use them sparingly
- Ask yourself: would a comma work here? If yes, use the comma
- Limit to 1-2 per paragraph maximum
- Never use multiple em dashes in the same sentence

## STRUCTURAL PATTERNS TO AVOID

### The AI Blog Post Template

AI defaults to this structure for everything:
1. Hook/intro paragraph
2. 3-4 body paragraphs with headers
3. Bullet point list
4. Conclusion summarizing the post

**Problem**: This structure is fine sometimes, but AI uses it for EVERYTHING.

**Fix**: Vary your structure. Sometimes skip the intro. Sometimes end abruptly. Sometimes use no headers at all.

### The Rule of Three Addiction

AI loves grouping things in threes:
- "Innovation, collaboration, and excellence"
- "Fast, efficient, and reliable"
- "Plan, execute, and deliver"

**Fix**: Use two things. Or four. Or just one. Real writing has variety.

### Moralizing Wrap-ups

AI always ends with a lesson or takeaway, even when the content doesn't warrant one.

**Fix**: Not everything needs a moral. Sometimes you can just... end.

### The "Journey" Framing

AI frames everything as a journey:
- "Our journey began..."
- "As we continue on this journey..."
- "Join us on this exciting journey..."

**Fix**: Just describe what happened. Skip the journey metaphor.

## AUTHENTICITY MARKERS (What TO Do)

### What AI Can't Replicate Well

1. **Specificity**: Concrete details from actual experience
   - Bad: "We had productive discussions"
   - Good: "We spent 45 minutes arguing about API versioning before anyone touched the whiteboard"

2. **Imperfection**: Real writing has personality quirks
   - Sentence fragments. On purpose.
   - Starting sentences with "And" or "But"
   - Occasional informality

3. **Actual opinions**: AI hedges everything
   - Bad: "There are various perspectives on this issue"
   - Good: "I think X is wrong, and here's why"

4. **Humor and self-deprecation**: AI plays it safe
   - Real humans make jokes that might not land
   - Real humans admit when something didn't go as planned

5. **Varied sentence length**:
   - AI writes medium-length sentences consistently
   - Real writing: Short. Then a longer one that builds on the idea and adds complexity. Then short again.

### Voice Consistency

Your writing should sound like YOU across posts. AI-assisted writers often have inconsistent voices because each prompt produces different output.

## THE ULTIMATE TEST

Read your writing out loud. Would you actually say this to a colleague? If it sounds like a press release or a corporate mission statement, rewrite it.

Real people don't "leverage synergies." They "work together." Real people don't "embark on journeys." They "start projects." Real people don't find things "transformative." They find things "useful" or "helpful" or sometimes just "good."

Write like a person talking to another person. That's it.
